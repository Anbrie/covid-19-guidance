Geographical distribution

A total of 725 cases of COVID-19 have been reported in Canada as of March 19, 2020 at 9:30 AM EST. These cases have been reported from all 10 provinces (Figure 1). No cases have been reported in the three territories.

Ten deaths have been reported.

Demographics

Of the COVID-19 cases reported in Canada to date, approximately half (54%) are male. Approximately one third (31%) of cases are 60 years old and over (Figure 4).

Symptoms and severity

Commonly reported symptoms among reported cases include cough (80%), fever (55%) and shortness of breath (26%).

Based on case reports received to date, 8% of cases have been hospitalized but did not require admission to the ICU and 3% required ICU admission. Note that the Public Health Agency of Canada does not receive updates on case hospitalization status.

Ten people have died of COVID-19 to date in Canada.
