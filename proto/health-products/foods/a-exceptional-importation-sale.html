Exceptional importation and sale of foods for a special dietary purpose

You may be eligible to import or sell this product through the Interim Order

The interim order may allow exceptional importation and sale of food products that are:

  • specially processed or formulated to meet the specific medical requirements of an individual
  • sole or primary source of nutrition for an individual

such as feeding-tube formulas, infant formulas, and human milk fortifiers.

Applying for authorization through the Interim Order

You need to submit a request to get the product "designated". To submit a request, contact the Bureau of Nutritional Sciences via phone at 613 948-8476 or via via email at hc.bns-bsn.sc@canada.ca

If we approve your request, your product will become designated and added to the List of foods for a special dietary purpose for exceptional importation and sale.

Importing a designated food product under the Interim Order

As an importer, you must:

  • for each time you import the product, notify the Minister at least five calendar days before the day of importation
    • Designated Food for a Special Dietary Purpose Notification Form
  • ensure that product information is available, in both English and French, for safe preparation and use of the food, including:
    • special dietary purpose for which the food is represented
    • warnings (if applicable)
    • directions for the preparation, use and storage of the food
    • expiration date
    • the lot number of the food
    • the list of food ingredients, including priority allergens
