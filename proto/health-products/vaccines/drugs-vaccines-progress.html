Distributing equipment to administer the vaccine

To efficiently, effectively and equitably distribute and administer an eventual COVID-19 vaccine, support is being given to:

  • Purchase materials to support safe efficient immunization such as syringes, needs, alcohol swabs and other supplies
  • Purchase equipment and supplies to perform the final manufacturing and packing stages of vaccine production in Canada
  • Assess feasibility and equity of options for storage and handling and administration
  • Accelerate production and testing of vaccine candidates by the National Research Council

Drugs, including vaccines must meet the regulatory requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they can be sold in Canada.

Drugs and treatment options also in clinical trials

Drugs and treatment options in Canadian clinical trials and research studies include:

  • antiviral drugs
  • anti-malarial drugs
  • monoclonal antibodies and other drugs that address inflammation
  • convalescent plasma collected from patients who have recovered from COVID-19
  • medical gases

Vaccines are safe to use and crucial to protecting Canadians

The Government of Canada has strict regulations and testing requirements to ensure that approved vaccines are safe to use.

Drugs, including vaccines must meet the regulatory requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they can be sold in Canada.

Working with international partners to ensure vaccines are safe and widely adopted

Canada is working with international partners on a coordinated and well-aligned approach to this global pandemic. This ensures that health products are safe, effective and quickly available to Canadians. Collaboration also helps advance the development of diagnostics, treatments and vaccines that will save lives and protect the health and safety of people everywhere.

  • Canada has committed to funding global vaccinations for COVID-19
