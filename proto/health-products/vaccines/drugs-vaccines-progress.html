COVID-19 vaccine progress tracker

79% of Canadians report via Statistics Canada they are likely to get a COVID-19 vaccination when it's available. Safe and effective drugs and vaccines are in progress to support Canadians through and out of this pandemic. Currently, there are no vaccines authorized to prevent, treat or cure COVID-19 in Canada.

Vaccine clinical trials

Clinical trials are crucial to ensuring that potential COVID-19 vaccines are safe for Canadians and effectively prevent infections. Canada's National Advisory Committee on Immunization (NACI) is providing rapid guidance on clinical trial research priorities for COVID-19 vaccine candidates.

Vaccine agreements

With advice from the COVID-19 Vaccine Task Force, Public Services and Procurement Canada is making agreements to secure millions of doses of potential vaccine candidates. All of the potential vaccines will require Health Canada regulatory approval before the vaccines can be delivered to Canadians. Active negotiations with other potential vaccine suppliers are also under way.

Planning ahead for vaccinations

Progress is being made towards the efficient, effective and equitable distribution and administration of an eventual COVID-19 vaccine. These efforts include:

  • Purchasing materials to support safe efficient immunization such as syringes, needs, alcohol swabs and other supplies Public Services and Procurement Canada
  • Purchasing equipment and supplies to perform the final manufacturing and packing stages of vaccine production in Canada
  • Feasibility and equity analyses of options for storage and handling and administration
  • Accelerate production and testing of vaccine candidatesby the National Research Council

Drug and therapeutic treatment progress tracker

Drugs and treatment options in Canadian clinical trials and research studies include:

  • antiviral drugs
  • anti-malarial drugs
  • monoclonal antibodies and other drugs that address inflammation
  • convalescent plasma collected from patients who have recovered from COVID-19
  • medical gases

Therapeutic research funding

The Therapeutics Task Force is providing expert advice to the Government on the development of COVID-19 treatments, including how best to prioritize financial support for promising COVID-19 treatment projects. The Task Force includes experts drawn from research and industry who have a proven ability to develop and bring new therapies to market in Canada.

Safe drugs and vaccines for Canada and the world

Drugs, including vaccines, are regulated under the Food and Drugs Act and Regulations. They must meet the regulatory requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they can be sold in Canada.

Learn more about Canada's COVID-19 regulatory process

We are also working with international partners on a coordinated and well-aligned approach to this global pandemic. This ensures that health products are safe, effective and quickly available to Canadians. Collaboration also helps advance the development of diagnostics, treatments and vaccines that will save lives and protect the health and safety of people everywhere.

Canada has committed to funding global vaccinations for COVID-19
