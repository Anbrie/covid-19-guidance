Vaccines and treatments approved for use in Canada

Health Canada scientific reviewers evaluate data to assess the potential benefits and risks of a drug or vaccine. They also review the information that will be provided to health care practitioners and consumers about the product. After the review, we may authorize the drug or vaccine for sale in Canada only if the benefits of the product outweigh the potential risks.

Securing potential vaccines and supplies

The Government of Canada is securing millions of doses of potential vaccine candidates. All potential vaccines will require Health Canada regulatory approval prior to being used to vaccinate Canadians.

Planning for manufacturing and immunization

Plans and purchases are being made to efficiently, effectively and equitably produce, distribute and administer an eventual COVID-19 vaccine. These include:

  • Accelerate production and testing of vaccine candidates by the National Research Council
  • Agreements to purchase syringes, alcohol swabs and other immunization supplies
  • Funding and agreements for final manufacturing and packing stages of vaccine production in Canada
  • Assess feasibility and equity of options for storage and handling and administration

Making sure vaccines and treatments are safe

All drugs, including vaccines, must meet the regulatory requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they can be sold in Canada. Phase 1, 2 and 3 clinical human trials ensure that potential COVID-19 vaccines are safe for Canadians and effectively prevent infections.

Supported by: Health Canada - Conducting COVID-19 clinical trials

Clinical trials of drugs in Canada

Drugs and treatment options in Canadian human trials and research studies include:

  • antiviral drugs
  • anti-malarial drugs
  • monoclonal antibodies and other drugs that address inflammation
  • convalescent plasma collected from patients who have recovered from COVID-19
  • medical gases

For a complete list, see Health Canada's authorized COVID-19 clinical trials

Vaccines are safe to use and crucial to protecting Canadians

The Government of Canada has strict regulations and testing requirements to ensure that approved vaccines are safe to use.

Drugs, including vaccines must meet the regulatory requirements for safety, efficacy and quality before they can be sold in Canada.

Working with international partners to ensure vaccines are safe and widely adopted

Canada is working with international partners on a coordinated and well-aligned approach to this global pandemic. This ensures that health products are safe, effective and quickly available to Canadians. Collaboration also helps advance the development of diagnostics, treatments and vaccines that will save lives and protect the health and safety of people everywhere.
