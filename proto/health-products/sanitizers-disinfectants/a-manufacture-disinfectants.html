Manufacture hard surface disinfectants in Canada

You must receive authorization from Health Canada’s Natural and Non-prescription Health Products Directorate

To obtain an authorization to market a disinfectant that meets the parameters in the monograph, please follow these steps:

Step 1: Ensure the hard surface disinfectant meets quality standards

You will need a certificate attesting that the quality of the product meets the standards in the

  • Hard Surface Disinfectants Monograph

AND

Step 2: Complete a Drug Submission Application Form

You will need to submit a Drug Submission Application form and get a Drug Identification Number (DIN) before you can manufacture and distribute a hard surface disinfectant in Canada.

  • Cover letter
  • Canadian label materials (e.g. labels, package inserts – as applicable)
  • If using the Regulatory Enrolment Process (REP)
    • Regulatory Transaction XML
    • Product Information XML (as required)
  • If not using the Regulatory Enrolment Process (REP)
    • Health Canada Drug Submission Application Form (3011)
    • Drug Submission - Application Fee Form for Human and Disinfectant Drugs

The following might be required in your application depending on your circumstances:

  • Non-prescription drug monograph attestation form
  • Drug Identification Number (DIN) Submission Certification for Human and Disinfectant Drugs
  • Letter of Authorization And Administrative Changes - Certification Form for Human and/or Disinfectant Drug Submissions and Applications

AND

Step 3: Submit your application package

Please see the Guidance Document: Management of Drug Submissions and the Management of Disinfectant Drug Applications that outlines the submission process.

For products outside the monograph, you are also required to:

  1. Submit
    • Product specific safety and efficacy evidence to support your product
    • A full study report

      • Note: all tests and studies must be conducted using Good Laboratory Practices (GLP)

  2. Meet the quality requirements as per the Disinfectant Drugs Guidance and Food and Drug Regulations
  3. Maintain the specifications for the product
  4. Have stability data on file to support the product’s shelf life

Details are found in the following guidance documents:

  • Guidance Document Non-Clinical Laboratory Study Data Supporting Drug Product Applications and Submissions: Adherence to Good Laboratory Practice
  • Management of Disinfectant Drug Applications
  • Guidance document: Disinfectant Drugs
  • Guidance Document: Safety and Efficacy Requirements for Surface Disinfectant Drugs
