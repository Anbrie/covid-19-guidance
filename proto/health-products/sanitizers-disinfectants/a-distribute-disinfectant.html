Manufacture disinfectants in Canada

You must meet the requirements of the Food and Drugs act and the Cosmetic Regulations

Cosmetic products sold in Canada must:

  • be safe to use and not pose any health risk
  • meet the requirements of the Food and Drugs Act (FDA) and the Cosmetic Regulations (CR)
    • consult the Cosmetic Ingredient Hotlist to see the list of ingredients that may conflict with the FDA and CR

You must:

  • manufacture, preserve, pack and store cosmetic products under sanitary conditions
  • ensure the manufacturer or importer notified Health Canada about the product
  • make sure the label of the cosmetic product includes:
    • an ingredient list
    • the identity of the product
    • the name and address of the manufacturer or importer
    • applicable warnings or cautions
    • directions for safe use of the product, if applicable

To understand all requirements to sell cosmetic products in Canada, consult:

  • Regulatory information for cosmetics and guidance documents

Products for use in food processing facilities

If the product label will indicate it can be used in food processing plants:

  • include the intended use in food processing facilities in your application
  • consider requesting a voluntary evaluation on the acceptability of the use of the product in food processing facilities
