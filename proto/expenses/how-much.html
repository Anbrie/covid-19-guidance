Overview

[intro goes here]

Size of the home

All finished areas within the home count towards the size of the home. This includes:

  • hallways
  • bathrooms
  • kitchen

You can measure in square metres or square feet.

Use of the work space

The work space calculation is affected by the:

  • Type of work space
  • Hours per week worked in the work space
  • Weeks or months of the year worked in the work space
  • Number of work spaces and workers in the home

  1. Determine the type of work space

    There are two types of work spaces:

    Common (shared) area

    A space that has other purposes besides your work (for example, working at a kitchen table). You claim the size of the entire room you work in.

    or

    Designated room

    A space designated solely for your work (for example, a home office).

    You can measure in square metres or square feet.

  2. Determine the hours per week worked in the work space

    The hours you can claim are based on whether the space is a common (shared) area or exclusively dedicated as a workspace.

    Common (shared) area

    This space can only be claimed for the hours it is used for work purposes. For example, if you work 40 hours a week at the dining table:

    •  40 hours (hours worked)
    • divided by 168 hours (total hours in a week)
    • times 100 (converting into a percentage)
    • equals 23.8% (percentage of time you work in the workspace)
    or

    Designated room

    This space can be claimed the full 24 hours per day during the claim period. For example, if you work 40 hours a week in your dedicated room:

    •  40 hours (hours worked)
    • equals 100% (percentage of time you can claim the workspace)

  3. Determine the weeks or months of the year worked in the work space

    You can only claim work space expenses for the same period of time you were required to work at home.

  4. Determine who claims the expenses

    If only one person has a work space

    If only one person in the home has a work space, that person must claim the expenses.

    If multiple people are working in the same home but different work spaces

    If multiple people are working in their own work space in the home, they each calculate and claim the expenses seperately.

    If multiple people are sharing the same work space

    If multiple people are sharing the work space, there are two options:

    Sharing the claim

    The amount of the claim can be divided up and shared between the workers, but the individual claims cannot exceed the total claim amount.

    or

    One person claims

    One person claims the full amount

Expense limitations

The expenses you can claim are limited when:

  • You have multiple income sources:
    • You can only deduct work space expenses from the income to which the expenses relate, and not from any other income.
  • Your expenses exceed your income:
    • The amount you can deduct for work-space-in-the-home expenses is limited to the amount of employment income remaining after all other employment expenses have been deducted. This means that you cannot use work space expenses to create or increase a loss from employment.
    • If you cannot deduct all your work space expenses in the year, you can carry forward the expenses. You can deduct these expenses in the following year as long as you are reporting income from the same employer. However, you cannot create or increase a loss from employment by carrying forward work space expenses.

Examples

Working in a designated room

Chris lives in a 7 room house and has a designated area in the home where Chris only performs employment duties.

  •  250 square feet (area of Chris' work space in his home)
  • divided by 2500 square feet (total finished area)
  • times 100 (converting into a percentage)
  • equals 10% (percentage of home Chris uses for work space)

If Chris was sharing this work space with someone (Danielle) who was also using the room as a dedicated work space, this gives them two options:

One person claims

Chris or Danielle could claim 10% of the work-space-in-the-home expenses such as water and electricity.

or

Both claim

Chris and Danielle could also share the claim but would only be able to claim a total of 10% of the expenses between the two of them.

Working in a common (shared) area

Sam and Terry rent an 8 room house. Due to COVID-19, they have been working from home using their dining room table.

  •  108 square feet (area used for work space in the home)
  • divided by 900 square feet (total finished area)
  • times 100 (converting into a percentage)
  • equals 12% (percentage of home you use for work space)

If 40 hours a week are spent working at the dining table:

  • 12% (percentage of home used for work space)
  • times 23.8% (40 hours worked divided by 168 total hours in a week)
  • equals 2.8%

This gives them two options:

One person claims

Sam or Terry could claim 2.8% of the work-space-in-the-home expenses such as water and electricity.

or

Both claim

Sam and Terry could also share the claim but would only be able to claim a total of 2.8% of the expenses between the two of them.
