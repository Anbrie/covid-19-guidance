Work space in the home expenses

Including employees working from home during COVID

This page has been updated to provide additional clarity on claiming the work space in the home expense for the following employee types:

  • Employees earning commission income
  • Employees earning a salary
  • Employees working from home during Covid 19 (refer to: COVID-19 examples)

On this page

  • What can be claimed
  • Eligibility criteria
  • Calculating and claiming your expense

What can be claimed

If you meet the eligibility criteria, certain home expenses can be claimed for a work space in the home.

Can be claimed

All salaried employees and commission employees can claim

  • electricity
  • heating
  • maintenance
  • heat
  • water
  • gas
  • home or renter’s insurance
  • cleaning supplies
  • snow removal
  • gardening
  • garbage fees
  • minor repair costs
  • rent
  • office supplies
  • personal cell phone that was also used for business
and

Commission employees can also claim

  • property taxes
  • home insurance

Reminder: Keep your receipts to support your expense claim

Cannot be claimed

Salaried employees, and commission employees cannot claim

  • mortgage interest
  • capital cost allowance

Eligibility criteria

To be eligible to deduct expenses you paid in 2020 for employment use of a work space in your home you must meet the following conditions:

  • Note: To be eligible to deduct expenses you paid during COVID-19, a formal contract of employment is not required, a verbal agreement between the employer and the employee will be accepted.

  • What is T2200 or T2200 (Short)?

    The Declaration of Conditions of Employment (T2200) is completed by the employer to certify that an employee is required to work from home and that any employment expenses not reimbursed by the employer may be claimed on their annual tax return.

    Note: Keep a copy for your records, in case we request it.

You need all of the above to be eligible to claim working from home expenses

You are eligible to claim working from home expenses

Examples

Working from home for 4 weeks or more due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Continuously working from home during that time

Working situation

Kumi’s employer required her to work from home during April and May 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

Result

Because Kumi mainly (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during a continuous period of nine weeks in 2020, she may be able to deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Partially working from home during that time

Working situation

Emma’s employer required her to work from home from April to October 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked an alternating schedule of three weeks at home and one week in the office. During the weeks Emma worked from home, she performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

Result

Because Emma principally (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during a continuous period of 31 weeks in 2020, she may be able to deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Working situation

Chiu’s employer required him to work from home some of the time from April to August in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During this period, Chiu worked on an alternating schedule of two days from home and three days from his employer’s office one week, and three days from home and two days from his employer’s office the following week. During the days Chiu worked from home, he performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office.

Result

Because Chiu did not principally (more than 50% of the time) work from home during that continuous period of 22 weeks in 2020 (i.e., he worked from home only 50% of the time during the period), he cannot deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period.

Working from home for less than 4 weeks due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Continuously working from home during that time

Working situation

Isaac’s employer required him to work from home for three weeks beginning in mid-March 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these three weeks, he worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office. Following this three week period, Isaac returned to his employer’s office for the rest of 2020 to perform all of his employment duties.

Result

Because Isaac did not principally work from home during a reasonably significant period in 2020 (i.e., he exclusively worked from home during a continuous period of only three weeks), he cannot deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period.

Designated room calculation

  1. Calculate the percentage of work space used in the home

    Take the area you use for work space in the home and divide that by the total finished area (including hallways, bathrooms, kitchens, etc.)

    Example

    Chris lives in a 7 room house and has a designated area in the home where Chris only performs employment duties.

    •  250 square feet (area of Chris' work space in his home)
    • divided by 2500 square feet (total finished area)
    • times 100 (converting into a percentage)
    • equals 10% (percentage of home Chris uses for work space)

  2. Calculate the personal use versus work use portion

    Since the entire work space was used only for work purposes, the entire space can be claimed.

    Example continued

    This home office is 10% of the total space of the home. Chris can claim 10% of the home expenses, like electricity and heat, as work-space-in-the-home expenses.

    If Chris was sharing this work space with someone (Danielle) who was also using the room as a dedicated work space, this gives them two options:

    One person claims

    Chris or Danielle could claim 10% of the work-space-in-the-home expenses such as water and electricity.

    or

    Both claim

    Chris and Danielle could also share the claim but would only be able to claim a total of 10% of the expenses between the two of them.

  3. Gather the bills

  4. Fill out the T777 or T777 (short)

    Enter the full amount of the bill on the appropriate line on the T777 Statement of Employment Expenses or T777C (short) Statement of Employment Expenses and then enter the total personal use portion on line 11 of the T777C (short)and line 37 of the T777.

    Use the new numbers to calculate the bills for workspace in the home.

    Use the same bills from the example or make new ones.

Common area calculation

  1. Calculate the percentage of work space used in the home

    Take the area used for work space in the home and divide that by the total finished area (including hallways, bathrooms, kitchens, etc.)

    Example

    Sam and Terry rent an 8 room house. Due to COVID-19, they have been working from home using their dining room table.

    •  108 square feet (area used for work space in the home)
    • divided by 900 square feet (total finished area)
    • times 100 (converting into a percentage)
    • equals 12% (percentage of home you use for work space)

  2. Calculate the personal use versus work use portion

    Half of the time the dining table is used for work and the other half for personal use.

    Example continued

    • 12% (percentage of home used for work space)
    • times 50% (percentage of time used for work)
    • equals 6%

    This gives them two options:

    One person claims

    Sam or Terry could claim 6% of the work-space-in-the-home expenses such as water and electricity.

    or

    Both claim

    Sam and Terry could also share the claim but would only be able to claim a total of 6% of the expenses between the two of them.

  3. Gather the bills

  4. Fill out the T777 or T777 (short)

    Enter the full amount of the bill on the appropriate line on the T777 Statement of Employment Expenses or T777C (short) Statement of Employment Expenses and then enter the total personal use portion on line 11 of the T777C (short)and line 37 of the T777.

    Use the new numbers to calculate the bills for workspace in the home.
