Examples

Working from home for more than 4 weeks due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Continuously working from home during that time

Working situation

Kumi’s employer required her to work from home during April and May 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

Result

Because Kumi principally (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during a continuous period of nine weeks in 2020, she may be able to deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Partially working from home during that time

Working situation

Emma’s employer required her to work from home from April to October 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked an alternating schedule of three weeks at home and one week in the office. During the weeks Emma worked from home, she performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

Result

Because Emma principally (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during a continuous period of 31 weeks in 2020, she may be able to deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Working situation

Chiu’s employer required him to work from home some of the time from April to August in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During this period, Chiu worked on an alternating schedule of two days from home and three days from his employer’s office one week, and three days from home and two days from his employer’s office the following week. During the days Chiu worked from home, he performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office.

Result

Because Chiu did not principally (more than 50% of the time) work from home during that continuous period of 22 weeks in 2020 (i.e., he worked from home only 50% of the time during the period), he cannot deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period.

Working from home for less than 4 weeks due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Continuously working from home during that time

Working situation

Isaac’s employer required him to work from home for three weeks beginning in mid-March 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these three weeks, he worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office. Following this three week period, Isaac returned to his employer’s office for the rest of 2020 to perform all of his employment duties.

Result

Because Isaac did not principally work from home during a reasonably significant period in 2020 (i.e., he exclusively worked from home during a continuous period of only three weeks), he cannot deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period.

What can be claimed

You can deduct the part of your costs that relates to your work space

Can be claimed
  • electricity
  • heating
  • maintenance
  • property taxes (commission employees only)
  • home insurance (commission employees only)
Cannot be claimed
  • mortgage interest
  • capital cost allowance
