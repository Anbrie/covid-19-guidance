    What is T2200 or T2200 (Short)

    One of these two forms is mandatory for the employee to claim eligible employment expenses not reimbursed by the employer on their annual tax return.

    T2200
    The Declaration of Conditions of Employment (T2200) is a form completed by the employer to certify that an employee is required to pay their own expenses in order to earn employment income. The employer also identifies on this form which expenses are required in order to carry out the duties of employment.
    T2200 Short
    The Declaration of Conditions of Employment for Working at Home During COVID-19 (T2200 Short) is a shorter version of the T2200 used specifically when employees were required to work from their home during COVID-19. It is completed by the employer to certify that an employee was required to pay their own expenses in order to earn employment income. The employer also identifies on this form the period where the employee worked from home during COVID-19 and the home related expenses that were required in order to carry out the duties of employment.

You need all of the above to be eligible to claim working from home expenses

You are eligible to claim working from home expenses

Examples

Working from home for 4 weeks or more due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Continuously working from home during that time

Working situation

Kumi’s employer required her to work from home during April and May 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Kumi was required to work from home for a reasonably significant period in 2020 (i.e., a continuous period of nine weeks) and she principally (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during that period, she may be able to deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Partially working from home during that time

Working situation

Emma’s employer required her to work from home from April to October 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these months, she worked an alternating schedule of three weeks at home and one week in the office. During the weeks Emma worked from home, she performed all of the duties that she normally performed at her employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Emma was required to work from home for a reasonably significant period in 2020 (i.e., a continuous period of 31 weeks) and she principally (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during that period, she may be able to deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Working situation

Jason is a part-time employee who works three days a week at his employer’s office. From mid-March to July 2020, Jason’s employer required him to work from home due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During this period, he performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office.

successful Result

Because Jason was required to work from home for a reasonably significant period in 2020 (i.e., a continuous period of 20 weeks) and he principally (more than 50% of the time) worked from home during that period, he may be able to deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period if the other conditions are met.

Working from home for less than 4 weeks due to the COVID-19 pandemic

Continuously working from home during that time

Working situation

Isaac’s employer required him to work from home for three weeks beginning in mid-March 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic. During these three weeks, he worked exclusively from home and performed all of the duties that he normally performed at his employer’s office. Following this three-week period, Isaac returned to his employer’s office for the rest of 2020 to perform all of his employment duties.

unsuccessful Result

Because Isaac was not required to work from home for a reasonably significant period in 2020 (i.e., his employer required him to work from home for a continuous period of only three weeks), he cannot deduct work-space-in-the-home expenses paid in that period.
