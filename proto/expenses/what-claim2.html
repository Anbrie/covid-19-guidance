Home expenses

If you meet the eligibility criteria, you can claim a portion of certain home expenses that support your work space in the home.

Commission employees, who typically have an amount in box 42 on their T4 slip, can claim additional expenses that salaried employees cannot claim.

Can be claimed

All salaried employees and commission employees can claim

Household expenses:

  • maintenance and minor repair costs Footnote 1
  • electricity
  • heat
  • water
  • utility (electricity, heat, and water) portion of condominium fees
  • rent Footnote 2

If used in the work space:

  • cleaning supplies
  • wall paint
  • light bulbs

Commission employees can also claim

  • property taxes
  • home insurance

Cannot be claimed

Salaried employees and commission employees cannot claim

  • mortgage interest
  • mortgage payments
  • furniture
  • wall decorations
  • capital cost allowance (the tax deduction for items that depreciate, such as furniture and equipment)
  • capital expenses (replacing windows, flooring, furnace etc)Footnote 3

Office supplies, internet, and phone expenses

If you are required to pay for office supplies or have connectivity expenses (phone or internet), as a condition of your employment, you may be able to claim those expenses.

Although these expenses can be claimed, they are not related to the physical work space in your home. They are claimed in a different section of the T777 form.

Office supplies

An office supply is an items that are used up directly in the performance of the duties of the employment. You can only claim the expense, or portion of the expense, that is used for work purposes.

Cell phone

You may deduct a portion of your basic cell phone service plan if all of the following conditions are met:

  • the cost of the plan is reasonable
  • you are able to substantiate the cellular minutes or data consumed directly in the performance of your employment duties (as well as the cost of the minutes or data)
  • the cost of the plan has been apportioned between employment and personal use on a reasonable basis

However, you cannot deduct amounts you paid to connect or license the cell phone.

Land line phone

You can deduct expenses you paid for long-distance telephone calls, as long as you paid them to earn employment income. However, you cannot deduct the monthly basic rate for a telephone.

Other employment expenses

You may be eligible to claim a deduction for employment expenses if you incurred any of the following expenses.

Salaried employees can claim

  • Accounting and legal fees
  • Allowable motor vehicle expenses
  • Travelling expenses
  • Parking
  • Salary expenses
  • Office Rent
  • Excess employees profit-sharing plan (EPSP) amounts

Commission employees can claim

  • Accounting and legal fees
  • Advertising and promotion
  • Allowable motor vehicle expenses
  • Food and beverages
  • Entertainment expenses
  • Lodging
  • Parking
  • Licences
  • Bonding premiums
  • Medical underwriting fees
  • Computers, cell phones, and other equipment
  • Salaries for an assistant
  • Office rent
  • Training costs
  • Travel fare
  • Excess employees profit-sharing plan (EPSP) amounts

Industry-specific

Certain employees can claim their expenses according to the industry they work in:

  • Transportation employees
  • Forestry operations
  • Employed artists
  • Employed tradespersons (including apprentice mechanics)

Limitations on expenses

The expenses you can claim are limited when:

  • You work only a portion of the year from your home:
    • You can claim the expenses that you paid in the portion of the year you worked from home. You cannot claim the expenses you paid for the whole year.
  • You have multiple income sources:
    • You can only deduct work space expenses from the income to which the expenses relate, and not from any other income.
  • Your expenses exceed your income:
    • The amount you can deduct for work-space-in-the-home expenses is limited to the amount of employment income remaining after all other employment expenses have been deducted. This means that you cannot use work space expenses to create or increase a loss from employment.
    • If you cannot deduct all your work space expenses in the year, you can carry forward the expenses. You can deduct these expenses in the following year as long as you are reporting income from the same employer. However, you cannot create or increase a loss from employment by carrying forward work space expenses.

Keep your records

Employees should keep the following records for six years:

  • Declaration of Conditions of Employment (Form T2200 or T2200 (Short))
  • any receipts, supporting documents and records

Maintenance and minor repair costs
The maintenance and minor repair costs generally fall into one of the following categories:

  • Expenses you paid that relate to the work space as well as other areas of the home. You can claim the percentage of those expenses that relate to the work space.
    • Example: Minor repairs of the home furnace or air-conditioner or the purchase of household cleaning products.
  • Expenses related to the work space only. You can deduct the total amount of the expenses if the amount paid is reasonable.
    • Example: Purchase of light bulbs, repainting the work space, or repairing walls or ceilings following the installation of phones, fax machines or other office equipment used in the work space.
  • Expenses related to a part of the house that was not used as a work space. You cannot claim any part of those expenses.
    • Example: Repainting a bedroom.

Rent

  • If you rent your home, you can claim a reasonable portion of the rent related to the work space.
  • If you own your home, you cannot claim the rental value of the work space in your home.

Capital expenses

  • The renovations and expenses that extend the useful life of a property or improve it beyond its original condition usually are capital expenses.
    • Example: Changing a furnace, changing a window or improving the flooring of a room.
