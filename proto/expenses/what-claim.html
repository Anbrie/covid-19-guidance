Household expenses

If you meet the eligibility criteria, you can claim a portion of certain household expenses.

Commission employees, who typically have an amount in box 42 on their T4 slip, can claim additional expenses that salaried employees cannot claim. Refer to: Criteria to be considered a commission employee

Can be claimed

All salaried employees and commission employees can claim

  • cleaning supplies
  • maintenance costs Footnote 1
  • heat
  • water
  • rent Footnote 2
and

Commission employees can also claim

  • property taxes
  • home insurance

Cannot be claimed

Salaried employees and commission employees cannot claim

  • mortgage interest
  • capital cost allowance
Footnote 1

Maintenance costs
If the expenses you paid (such as cleaning materials or paint) were to maintain:

  • the whole house, then you may be able to deduct a portion of them
  • the work space only, then you may be able to deduct all or most of them
  • a part of the house that was not used as a work space, then you cannot deduct any part of them

Keep your records

Employees should keep the follow records for six years:

  • Declaration of Conditions of Employment (Form T2200)
  • any receipts, supporting documents and records

Employment expenses not related to your work space

If you are required to purchase other items as a condition of your employment, you may be able to claim those expenses. Examples include:

  • Costs pertaining to phones (airtime, long distance calls)
  • Internet (commission employees only)
  • Supplies (stationery items, stamps, toner, ink cartridges, etc.)
  • Vehicle (fuel, parking, payments, insurance, etc. )

However, these types of expenses not related to your work space in the home and are claimed a different way. Refer to:

  • Salaried employees - Employment expenses
  • Commissioned employees - Employment expenses
