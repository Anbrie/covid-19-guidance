Expenses

Household expenses

If you meet the eligibility criteria, you can claim a portion of certain household expenses.

Commission employees, who will have an amount in box 42 on their T4 slip, can claim additional expenses that salaried employees cannot claim. Refer to: Criteria to be considered a commission employee

Can be claimed

All salaried employees and commission employees can claim

  • cleaning supplies
  • maintenance costs Footnote 1
  • heat
  • water
  • natural gas
  • home or renter’s insurance
  • garbage fees
  • rent
and

Commission employees can also claim

  • property taxes
  • home insurance

Cannot be claimed

Salaried employees and commission employees cannot claim

  • mortgage interest
  • capital cost allowance
Footnote 1

Maintenance costs: It may not be appropriate to use a percentage of these costs. For example, if the expenses you paid (such as cleaning materials or paint) were to maintain a part of the house that was not used as a work space, then you cannot deduct any part of them. Alternatively, if the expenses you paid were to maintain the work space only, then you may be able to deduct all or most of them.

Other expenses

If you are required to purchase other items as a condition of your employment, you may be able to claim those expenses. Examples include:

  • Costs pertaining to phones (purchase, air time, basic plans, airtime, licensing, etc.)
  • Internet (commission employees only)
  • Supplies (stationery items, stamps, toner, ink cartridges, etc.)
  • Vehicle (fuel, parking, payments, insurance, etc. )

However, these types of expenses not related to your work space in the home and are claimed a different way. Refer to:

  • Salaried employees - Employment expenses
  • Commissioned employees - Employment expenses

Keep your records

Employees should keep the follow records for six years:

  • Declaration of Conditions of Employment (Form T2200)
  • any receipts, supporting documents and records

Expenses when there are multiple income sources

You can only deduct work space expenses from the income to which the expenses relate, and not from any other income.

Expenses that exceed your income

The amount you can deduct for work-space-in-the-home expenses is limited to the amount of employment income remaining after all other employment expenses have been deducted. This means that you cannot use work space expenses to create or increase a loss from employment.

If you cannot deduct all your work space expenses in the year, you can carry forward the expenses. You can deduct these expenses in the following year as long as you are reporting income from the same employer. However, you cannot create or increase a loss from employment by carrying forward work space expenses.

Size of spaces

Size of home

All finished areas within the home count towards the size of the home. This includes:

  • hallways
  • bathrooms
  • kitchen
  • basements
  • sunrooms

You can measure in square metres or square feet.

Size of work space

You can only choose one principle room where you work from, even if you move around throughout the day. The only exception is if you have a seperate room that you meet clients in, then you can claim that as well. There are two types of work spaces:

Common (shared) area

A space that has other purposes besides your work (for example, working at a kitchen table). You claim the size of the entire room you work in. However, since this is a common space, you will prorate the space, based on the number of hours you work in it.

Example: If you work 40 hours a week at the kitchen table, claim the entire size of the kitchen. However, you cannot claim this space as 100% for work purposes. Instead, you would have to calculate the percentage use (40 work hours/168 hours in a week = 23.8%).

or

Designated room

A space designated solely for your work (for example, a home office).

Example: If you work 40 hours a week at your home office, claim the entire size of the home office. You would claim this space as 100% for work purposes.

You can measure in square metres or square feet.
