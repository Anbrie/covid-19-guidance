COVIDAlert - COVID-19 exposure notification app

Help stop the spread of COVID-19. Protect your friends, family and community by downloading Health Canada’s mobile exposure notification app.

Protect yourself, protect others. Get notified.

COVIDAlert alerts you if someone you've been near tells the app they tested positive.

That way, you can isolate right away and stop the spread.

The app uses Bluetooth to exchange anonymous random codes with nearbye phones.

How it works

Exposure notification via Bluetooth, between smartphones that are within range and which have the application installed. Both phones broadcast and store these random codes for 14 days.

Every day, the app checks a list of random codes from people who report a COVID-19 diagnosis through the app.

If you've been near one of those codes in the past 14 days, you'll get a notification that you've been exposed.

Random codes are flagged as COVID-19 exposures when someone tests positive

A randomly generated code of digits and letters that changes every 5 minutes is sent over Bluetooth whenever another phone comes into range. You control what that range is in your app settings.

The app estimates how far away other phones are with the strength of their Bluetooth signal. It does not use or access any location data.

Only if you test positive for COVID-19, you can choose to upload all the codes your phone sent out during the last 14 days. The codes go into a central server.

Every other phone checks the server regularly to see if any of the random codes they recorded have tested positive for COVID-19 and, if so, notify their users they were exposed.

How Bluetooth works and when a signal could be weakened

Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology and there are many factors that can influence the strength of its signal: metals, walls, and even other devices including microwave ovens. In addition, Bluetooth technology can share its frequency with some Wi-Fi networks, which can result in a slower connection. Unfortunately, this type of interference cannot be completely eliminated and these factors are beyond the ability of any app to control.

In general, it is best that the distance between the two devices does not exceed 10 meters.

Completely private, completely anonymous

COVIDAlert:

  • Only the anonymous codes are collected and stored for 14 days. They are shared with the server only when a one-time positive test number is entered.
  • It has no way of knowing any of your personal information (name, email, phone contacts, health information, etc)
  • It can't know, use or access your location data (GPS) in any way
  • Nothing is done without your permission.
Random codes cannot be connected with you

The random code is a randomly generated string of digits and letters. It changes every 5 minutes so it cannot be used to identify you.

Only if you test positive for COVID-19, you can choose to upload all the codes your phone sent out during the last 14 days. The codes go into a central server. The sever only gets the codes. It does not get any information about the person.

The only information shared in this notification service is the random codes.

The random codes are automatically deleted after 14 days from all devices and the server.

Nothing is done without your permission

Your phone keeps the date, duration and signal strength related all the random codes it collects. But those details are never shared outside your phone.

The app will ask you to allow it to log random codes. If you want to use COVIDAlert, you need to give permission.

Full details in the privacy policy

Notify others with your positive test number

If you test positive for COVID-19, your healthcare provider will give you a one-time 8-digit number that lets you flag any random codes you gave out in the past 14 days.

These random codes are then automatically retrieved by other phones. When there is a match the app will give the user an exposure notification.

Not all provinces are giving out positive test numbers yet

Ontario will be the first to distribute the 8-digit numbers. The Government of Canada is working with other provinces and territories to make this available across Canada soon.

Get COVIDAlert

The app is currently supported on any iOS or Android devices.

Alternative app for Alberta

Get the alternative Alberta exposure notification app for use in Alberta only instead.

Check for symptoms if you were exposed

Just because you were in proximity of someone with COVID-19, does not necessarily mean you were infected.

If your app notifies you that you were exposed to COVID-19, you should wash your hands regularly, socially distance yourself and check for symptoms.

If you do start feeling ill, you should:

  • Take a self-assessment and follow the next steps given

The more Canadians use the app, the safer we'll all be

Quickly identifying if we've been exposed to COVID-19 and knowing when to get tested or take precautions to prevent others from getting infected are important parts of limiting the spread of COVID-19.

The more people who voluntarily download and use the app in Canada, the more effective it becomes, the healthier we'll be, the faster we can hug our loved ones, reopen our economy, and stop COVID-19.

Share the app with your friends and family to ensure that we all protect ourselves and help keep others safe.
