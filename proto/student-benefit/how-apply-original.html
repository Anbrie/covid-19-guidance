Getting ready to apply (opens April x)

To ensure your application will be as easy and quick as possible, here is how to get started:

  1. Get a CRA My Account to apply online

    For faster service, apply online instead of on the phone

    Do you have a CRA My Account?

    • Yes

      Great, you can apply.

      Do you have any issues with logging in?

      • No

        Do you want step-by-step walkthrough of what information you will need to enter?

        • No
      • Yes I am locked out
      • Yes cannot remember my CRA userID or password
      • Yes I am getting error messages

        What is the error message?

        • Attempting to register but you have reached maximum number of credentials allowed (CER.015)
        • Issue with entering either the user ID and/or password (CER.018)
        • Security questions and answers do not match (CER.038)
        • Failed attempts at user ID recovery (CER.039)
        • Issue with validating identity during registration (ERR.018)
        • Attempted 5 times to validate identity and account is locked (ERR.019)
        • SIN not found (ERR.038)
        • You are going through identity authentication and the current and first prior year T1 tax return have not been assessed (ERR.062)
        • The error is not listed

  2. Set-up direct deposit

    The CESB is paid by direct deposit in 3 business days. If you don’t, you should get a cheque in about 10 business days.

    Is your direct deposit information up to date with the CRA?

    • Yes

      Great. This will ensure your benefit payment will be delivered to you.

    • No

      Sign in to your My CRA Account to update your direct deposit information.
