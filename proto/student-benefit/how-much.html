How much you can get

On this page

  • Graduating high school students
  • Post-secondary students
  • Tax implications

Graduating high school students

High school graduates are only able to apply for 2 eligibility periods, starting from the July 5 to August 1 eligibility period.

Graduating high-school students amount details
Amount Eligibility period
$1,250 or $1,750 depending on your eligibility July 5, 2020 to August 1, 2020
$1,250 or $1,750 depending on your eligibility August 2, 2020 to August 29, 2020

Post-secondary students

Eligible post-secondary students, post-secondary graduates, and students who have recently left their post-graduate studies are able to apply for 4 eligibility periods, starting from the May 10 to June 6 eligibility period.

Post-secondary students amount details
Amount Eligibility period
$1,250 or $1,750 depending on your eligibility May 10, 2020 to June 6, 2020
$1,250 or $1,750 depending on your eligibility June 7, 2020 to July 4, 2020
$1,250 or $1,750 depending on your eligibility July 5, 2020 to August 1, 2020
$1,250 or $1,750 depending on your eligibility August 2, 2020 to August 29, 2020

Tax implications

The CESB is a taxable benefit. You will need to report any payments you received on next year's income tax return. An information slip will be made available for the 2020 tax year in CRA My Account under Tax Information Slips (T4 and more).
