Graduating high school students

You can only apply for the CESB after graduating. Your first eligibility period will be the month after you have graduated.

For high school students graduating in June 2020, you can only apply for 2 eligibility periods, starting with the July 5 to August 1 eligibility period.

If you graduated high school before June 2020, you may be able to apply for eligibility periods in the Post-secondary students section. For example, if you graduated in May 2020, you can apply for the June 7 to July 4 eligibility period.

It provides a payment of $1,250 for a 4-week period or $2,000 if you have dependants or a disability.

Post-secondary students

Eligible post-secondary students, post-secondary graduates, and students who have recently left their post-graduate studies are able to apply for 4 eligibility periods, starting from the May 10 to June 6 eligibility period.

It provides a payment of $1,250 for a 4-week period from May through August 2020, or $2,000 for a 4-week period if you have dependants or a disability.

Impact on your taxes

The CESB is a taxable benefit. You will need to report any payments you received on next year's income tax return. An information slip will be made available for the 2020 tax year in CRA My Account under Tax Information Slips (T4 and more).
