Who can apply

You cannot apply for the CESB if you already applied for the CERB or EI

If you have already applied, or are receiving support from the Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) or Employment Insurance (EI,) you are not eligible to apply for the CESB.

Eligibility

To be eligible for the CESB, students must meet the following conditions:

    • Who is considered a protected person

      An individual who is recognized by the Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada (IRB) and Immigration Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) as person in need of protection.

    Recognized post-secondary educational programs and definitions
    • post-secondary level courses (from a university, college, or technical or vocational training at the secondary level in Quebec) that are at least 12-weeks in duration and are needed to obtain a degree, diploma, or certificate
    • institutions listed on:
      • Master List of designated educational institutions
      • Master List of certified educational institutions
      • Quebec’s directory of educational institutions and programs (universities, colleges, and secondary level vocational training)
    • Indigenous institutions recognized by their province
    • Register with the Job Bank
      • Current eligibility period: May 10 to June 6, 2020
      • Next eligibility period: June 7 to July 4, 2020

You are eligible for $1,250 per 4-week(s) and ready to proceed

Eligibility conditions for the benefit top-up

If you meet all of the conditions above, you may also be eligible for an additional $750 per payment.

    • Disability as defined for this benefit
      • A physical, mental, intellectual, cognitive, learning, communication or sensory impairment, or a permanent, temporary or episodic functional limitation that hinders a person’s full and equal participation in society
    • Who can be claimed as a dependant
      • a child (adopted child, stepchild, or foster child) who is under 18 years old and is completely dependent on the student, student’s spouse, or common law partner for support, care, and upbringing
      • a person who is completely dependent on the student, student’s spouse, or common law partner for support, care, and upbringing

You are eligible for $2,000 per 4-week(s) and ready to proceed

Verifying your eligibility

The CRA will verify that you are eligible to receive the CESB. If you apply but are not eligible, we will contact you to make arrangements to repay any amounts you may owe.
