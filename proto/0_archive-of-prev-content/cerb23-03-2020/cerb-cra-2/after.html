Determine the right benefit for you

If you continue to need income support after the CERB ends, find out if you qualify for the new simplified Employment Insurance (EI) program, effective September 27, 2020.

For those not eligible for EI, mainly the self-employed and gig economy workers, the new Canada Recovery Benefit would provide a benefit amount of $400 per week for up to 26 weeks.

EI or Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB)

  1. First, determine if you qualify for Employment Insurance (EI)

    Service Canada is temporarily changing the EI rules to allow more people affected by COVID-19 to qualify.

    You may get a minimum rate of $400 per week for up to 26 weeks

    Review simplified eligibility for EI

    If you qualify for EI, you may apply through Service Canada for it after September 27, 2020

    More information coming soon.

  2. Second, if you don’t qualify for EI, the Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB) may be right for you.

    The CRB will provide income support to workers who are self-employed or are not eligible for Employment Insurance (EI)

    This benefit is pending the passage of legislation in the House of Commons and Senate.

    Review draft eligibility for the CRB

    This benefit would be effective from September 27 for one year.

    If you qualify for CRB, you may apply through CRA for it after September 27, 2020

    This benefit is pending the passage of legislation in the House of Commons and Senate. More information coming soon.

Caregiving and sickness recovery benefits

If you’re not able to work due to your health or caregiving responsibilities related to COVID-19, you may be eligible for one of these other recovery benefits.

These benefits are pending the passage of legislation in the House of Commons and Senate.

Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit (CRCB)

The CRCB would provide income support ($500 per week, for up to 26 weeks per household) to employed and self-employed Canadians who are not able to work because they are caring for dependants, such as a young child or family member, because their regular school, facility or program is closed due to COVID-19.

Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB)

The CRSB would provide $500 per week for up to two weeks, for workers who cannot work because they are sick or must self-isolate for reasons related to COVID-19.
