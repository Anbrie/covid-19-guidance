When and how to expect your payment

If you are eligible for the CERB, you can expect $2,000 ($500 per week) for a pre-set 4-week period.

The CERB is paid by direct deposit in 3 business days if you have direct deposit with the CRA. If you don’t, you should get a cheque in about 10 business days.

The CERB is a taxable

You will need to report any payments you received on next year's tax return. An information slip will be made available for the 2020 tax year in My Account under Tax Information Slips (T4 and more).

Re-apply for the CERB

If your situation continues, you can re-apply for the CERB again for another 4-week period, up to a maximum of 4 periods (16 weeks in all).

If you start working again after you get a CERB payment, and then stop working, you need to re-apply for the CERB. When you re-apply, you must confirm that for at least 14 days in a row, during the period you are applying for, you won't earn:

  • employment income
  • self-employment income
  • provincial or federal benefits related to maternity or paternity leave

Determine when to re-apply

Return or repay the CERB

You may want to return or repay the CERB if you:

  • return to work earlier than expected
  • applied for CERB but later realize you’re not eligible

You have the option to return or repay your CERB payment now.

Be sure to:

  1. make payment out to "Receiver General for Canada"
  2. indicate it is for "Repayment of CERB"
  3. include your Social Insurance Number (SIN) or your Temporary Tax Number (TTN)
