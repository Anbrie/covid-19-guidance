Find out what support you can apply for during the COVID-19 outbreak: Taking care of someone or in quarantine

You can:

  • Apply for Canada Emergency Benefits
  • Make sure to file your tax return as soon as possible, even if you didn't have any income
  • Get mortgage or loan relief
  • Withdraw less from your Retirement Income Funds

Apply for Canada Emergency Benefits

If you are in quarantine, looking after children affected by school or daycare closures or taking care of someone who has COVID-19, you probably qualify for Canada Emergency Benefits.

The Emeregncy Care Benefit will be available starting in April 2020.

How to apply for the Canada Emergency Benefit

Make sure to file your tax return as soon as possible, even if you didn't have any income

To receive the GST Credit or Canada Child Benefit, you must have filed your tax return to the Canada Revenue Agency, even if you didn't receive any income.

May 2020 GST Credit payment

The Goverment of Canada Government is proposing to provide a one-time special payment by early May 2020 through the Goods and Services Tax credit (GSTC). The average boost to income for those benefitting from this measure will be close to $400 for single individuals and close to $600 for couples.

Increase to Canada Child Benefit payments

The Government is proposing to increase the maximum annual Canada Child Benefit (CCB) payment amounts, only for the 2019-20 benefit year, by $300 per child.

Tax payment deferral

The Canada Revenue Agency will allow all taxpayers to defer, until after August 31, 2020, the payment of any income tax amounts that become owing on or after today and before September 2020.

Get mortgage or loan relief

Student Loans

Student loans will have a 6 month payment deferral during which no interest will accrue.

Mortgages and other loans

Canadian banks have affirmed their commitment to working with customers to provide flexible solutions during the disruption period. You may be eligible to receive:

  • up to a 6-month payment deferral for mortgages
  • loan re-amortization
  • capitalization of outstanding interest arrears
  • a payment arrangement

Withdraw less from your Retirement Income Funds

The Government of Canada is reducing required minimum withdrawals from Registered Retirement Income Funds (RRIFs) by 25% for 2020.

This will provide flexibility to seniors that are concerned that they may be required to liquidate their RRIF assets to meet minimum withdrawal requirements. Similar rules would apply to individuals receiving variable benefit payments under a defined contribution Registered Pension Plan.
