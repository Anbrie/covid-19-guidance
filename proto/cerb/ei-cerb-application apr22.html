Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)

The Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) gives financial support to employed and self-employed Canadians who are directly affected by COVID-19.

If you are eligible, you can receive $2,000 for a 4-week period (the same as $500 a week).

CERB is jointly delivered by Service Canada and the Canada Revenue Agency. Only apply for the CERB through Service Canada or the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) – not both.

If you are eligible for Employment Insurance

You can follow the standard EI application process. If your application is accepted, you will receive CERB benefits as long as CERB lasts, after which you’ll begin receiving EI benefits.

  • It will take longer to process your application, and so longer to receive money
    • You won’t have to reapply for each CERB period (every 4 weeks)
  • You will have to report your employment status every 2 weeks to continue receiving benefits
  • You automatically receive EI once CERB ends
If you’re not eligible for Employment Insurance

Many people who aren’t eligible for EI can still receive CERB.

  • Apply on the phone or from within your CRA My Account
  • You will receive money within days (if you’re set up for direct deposit)
  • You must re-apply for each benefit period (every 4 weeks)
  • If you are eligible for EI, you must apply for it separately once CERB ends

Not sure whether to apply with Service Canada or the CRA?

Answer a few simple questions.

Thinking about your main source of income in the last year - were you an employee or self-employed?

  • I was self-employed

    Thank you! You should prepare to apply through the CRA.

  • I was an employee

    Were you a full-time employee for at least four months last year? (“full-time” means someone who works 30 or more hours per week)

    • Yes

      You should apply for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit through Service Canada and the Employment Insurance program.

    • No

      Were you a part-time employee for at least eight months last year? (part-time means someone who works less than 30 hours per week)

      • Yes

        You should apply for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit through Service Canada and the Employment Insurance program.

      • No

        Thank you! You should prepare to apply through the CRA.
