Understanding your Employment Insurance options

There are many differences between Canada's temporary Emergency Benefits and Employment Insurance:

  • Eligibility for Employment Insurance has changed to a minimum of 420 hours no matter where you live. Find out if you are eligible
  • Applying for the CERB and CESB didn't require providing documents to prove you were out of work. Documentation is required when you apply for EI.
  • Employment Insurance does not have eligibility periods. Instead you report every two weeks on your efforts to find work.
  • Emergency benefits for each eligibility period were paid soon after you applied. Employment Insurance benefits are paid after the period of your claim. So if you were unable to find work from November 1 to November 14, you will be paid for that period after November 14th.
  • Most people received the same CERB and CESB payment amounts. Employment Insurance is paid as a percentage based on your best weeks of insurable income.
