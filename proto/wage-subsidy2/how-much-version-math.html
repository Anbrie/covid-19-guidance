How the subsidy is calculated

For all claim periods, the CEWS amount is based on:

  • the number and type of eligible employees you have, and
  • the amount and type of pay they received

For claim periods 5 and later, the CEWS is also determined by the rate of eligible revenue reduction you experienced.

There is no limit on the total subsidy amount that an eligible employer may claim.

Calculation for claim periods 1 to 4 (March to June, 2020)

For each qualifying period, A-B-C+D, where:

A =

The total amount for all eligible employees for each week in the qualifying period, the greater of X or Y, where:

X =

The least of the following:

  • 75% of eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
  • $847, or
  • $0 – if non-arm's-length employee 
Y =

The least of the following:

  • eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
  • 75% of baseline remuneration for employee for that week
  • $847
B =
Amount calculated for the 10% Temporary Wage Subsidy for Employers  (must be the eligible amount for the period, even if the employer did not reduce their related payroll remittances by that amount)
C =
Total of amounts received by employees through ESDC's Work-Sharing program for any weeks in the qualifying period
D =

If the employer has any eligible employees on leave with pay :

Total amount of employers premium (EI) and employer CPP or QPP contributions paid in respect to those employees for any weeks in the qualifying period

and

Total amount of employer premiums paid under the Quebec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) in respect to those employees for any weeks in the qualifying period.

Calculation for claim periods 5 and 6 (July and August, 2020)

For each qualifying period, A-B+C, where:

A =
the total amount for all eligible employees for each week in the qualifying period:

  • if the eligible employee is active (not on leave with pay for that week), the greater of (A) or (B), where
    • (A) = either
      • 0, if the revenue reduction percentage of the qualifying entity for the period is less than 30%, or
      • in any other case, the greater of [the amount determined under subparagraph (a)(i)] and [the amount determined under subparagraph (a)(ii)]
      and
    • (B) = the amount determined by the formula (E + F) × G, where:
      • E is the eligible employer’s base rate for the period
      • F is the eligible employer’s top-up rate for the period, and
      • G is the least of:
        • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to the eligible employee by the qualifying entity in respect of that week
        • $1129, and
        • for non-arm’s-length employees, the baseline remuneration in respect of the eligible employee determined for that week
  • if the eligible employee is on leave with pay for that week (furloughed):
    • 0, unless
      • the revenue reduction rate for the period is greater than 0%, or
      • the top-up rate for the period is greater than 0%, and
    • in any other case, the greater of [the amount determined under subparagraph (a)(i)] and [the amount determined under subparagraph (a)(ii)] the least of:
      • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to the eligible employee in respect of that week
      • an amount determined by regulation in respect of the qualifying entity for the qualifying period
      • 0, if:
        • the employees is non-arm’s length , and
        • the baseline remuneration of the eligible employee for that week is 0
        and
      • 0, unless:
        • the revenue reduction rate for the period is greater than 0%, or
        • the top-up rate for the period is greater than 0%
B =
the total of amounts received by employees through ESDC's Work-Sharing program for any weeks in the qualifying period
C =

if the employer has any eligible employees on leave with pay:

The total amount of employers premium (EI) and employer CPP or QPP contributions paid in respect to those employees for any weeks in the qualifying period

and

The total amount of employer premiums paid under the Quebec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP) in respect to those employees for any weeks in the qualifying period.

Calculation for furloughed employees

For periods 5 and 6, the subsidy calculation for furloughed employees works the same way as the general employee calculation for periods 1 to 4. It is the greater of:

  • for arm’s-length employees, 75% of the amount of remuneration paid, up to a maximum benefit of $847 per week, and
  • the lesser of: 75% of the employee’s pre-crisis weekly remuneration up to a maximum benefit of $847 per week or the amount of remuneration paid [during the claim period?]

[the elimination of the 14-day rule]

[For period 5 and subsequent periods, the CEWS for furloughed employees is available to eligible employers that qualify for either the base rate or the top-up for active employees in the relevant period.]

The employer portion of contributions in respect of the Canada Pension Plan, Employment Insurance, the Quebec Pension Plan, and the Quebec Parental Insurance Plan in respect of furloughed employees would continue to be refunded to the employer.

Choosing to use the rules and calculations from period 4 (the "safe harbour" option)

For periods 5 and 6, you are entitled to the higher CEWS rate between two calculation options:

  • the amount calculated using the rules for the current period (period 5 or 6), or
  • the amount calculated using the rules that were in place for periods 1 to 4

This means that in periods 5 and 6, if you had a revenue decline of 30% or more you would receive an overall CEWS rate of at least 75%. It is possible you could receive an overall rate of up to 85% if you qualify for the top-up amount (60% maximum base rate + 25% maximum top-up rate).
