How the subsidy is calculated

For all claim periods, the CEWS amount is based on:

  • the number and type of eligible employees you have, and
  • the amount and type of pay they received

For claim periods 5 and later, the CEWS is also determined by the rate of eligible revenue reduction you experienced.

There is no limit on the total subsidy amount that an eligible employer may claim.

Calculation for claim periods 1 to 4 (March to June, 2020)

For each claim period, A-B-C+D, where:

A =

The total of all amounts—each of which is for an eligible employee in respect of a week in the claim period—equal to the greater of E or F, where:

E =

The least of the following:

  • 75% of the eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
  • $847, or
  • $0 – if non-arm's-length employee
F =

The least of the following:

  • the eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
  • 75% of the baseline remuneration of the employee determined for that week
  • $847
B =
The total of all amounts claimed (or intended to be claimed) for the 10% Temporary Wage Subsidy for Employers in the claim period
C =
The total of any work-sharing benefit amounts received by eligible employees during the claim period through ESDC's Work-Sharing program
D =
For an eligible employee for each whole week in the claim period that the employee was leave with pay, the total of the employer contributions to:

  • Employment Insurance (EI)
  • Quebec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP)
  • Canada Pension Plan (CPP)
  • Quebec Pension Plan (QPIP)
Calculation for claim periods 5 and 6 (July and August, 2020)

For each qualifying period, A-B+C, where:

A =
The total of all amounts—each of which is for an eligible employee in respect of a week in the qualifying period:
  • if the eligible employee is active (not on leave with pay for that week), the greater of E or F, where
    • E = either G or H, whichever applies, where
      • G = $0, if eligible employer’s revenue reduction percentage for the claim period is less than 30.00%, and
      • H = the greater of I or J, where
        • I = the least of the following:
          • 75% of the eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
          • $847, or
          • $0 – if non-arm's-length employee
          , and
        • J = the least of the following:
          • the eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
          • 75% of the baseline remuneration of the employee determined for that week; or
          • $847
      , and
    • F = the eligible employer’s overall CEWS rate for the period, multiplied by the least of:
      • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
      • $1129, and
      • If the employee is a non-arm's-length employee, the baseline remuneration of the employee determined for that week

      • or

    • if the employee is on leave with pay for that week (furloughed), either K or L, whichever applies, where:
      • K = $0, if the eligible employer’s revenue reduction rate and its top-up rate for the period are both 0%
      • L = the greater of M or N, where
        • M = the least of the following:
          • 75% of the eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
          • $847, or
          • $0 – if non-arm's-length employee
          and
        • N = the least of the following:
          • the eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
          • 75% of the baseline remuneration of the employee determined for that week; or
          • $847
B =
The total of any work-sharing benefit amounts received by eligible employees during the claim period through ESDC's Work-Sharing program
C =
For an eligible employee for each whole week in the claim period that the employee was leave with pay, the total of the employer contributions to:

  • Employment Insurance (EI)
  • Quebec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP)
  • Canada Pension Plan (CPP)
  • Quebec Pension Plan (QPIP)

Calculation for employees on leave with pay for periods 5 and 6

For periods 5 and later, you can calculate an amount of subsidy for eligible employees who were on leave with pay as long as your revenue reduction rate or top-up rate for the claim period is greater than 0%.

The employer portion of contributions in respect of the Canada Pension Plan, Employment Insurance, the Quebec Pension Plan, and the Quebec Parental Insurance Plan in respect of employees on leave with pay continue to be refunded for these periods.

These amounts are reflected in the calculation above.

Choosing to use the rules and calculations from period 4 (the "safe harbour" option)

For periods 5 and 6, if you have an eligible revenue drop of at least 30%, you are entitled to the higher CEWS rate between two calculation options:

  • the amount calculated using the rules for the current period (period 5 or 6), or
  • the amount calculated using the rules that were in place for periods 1 to 4

This means that in periods 5 and 6, if you had a revenue decline of 30% or more, you would receive an overall CEWS rate of at least 75%.

If you use the online calculator or spreadsheet to calculate your subsidy amount, the safe harbour option is automatically calculated based on information you entered, and applied if it is to your benefit.

Read more about how the revenue drop and subsidy rate are calculated for a claim period.

Calculation for claim periods 7 to 9 (September to November, 2020)

For each qualifying period, A-B+C, where:

A =
  • the total of all amounts—each of which is for an eligible employee in respect of a week in the qualifying period:
    • if the employee is active (not on leave with pay for that week), the amount determined by the eligible employer’s overall CEWS rate for the period, multiplied by the least of:
      • the amount of eligible remuneration paid to the eligible employee in respect of that week
      • $1129, and
      • If the employee is a non-arm’s-length employee, the baseline remuneration of the eligible employee determined for that week
      or
    • if the employee is on leave with pay (furloughed) for that week, the least of the following:
      • the eligible remuneration paid to the employee in respect of that week
      • the eligible employer’s amount determined by regulation for the claim period
      • $0, if either of the following applies
        • the employee is a non-arm’s-length employee and the baseline remuneration of the employee determined for that week is $0;
          or
        • the eligible employer’s revenue reduction rate and top-up rate for the period are both 0%
B =
The total of any work-sharing benefit amounts received by eligible employees during the claim period through ESDC's Work-Sharing program
C =
  • If the eligible employer’s revenue reduction rate or top-up rate are greater than 0%*, the total of the employer contributions to:
    • Employment Insurance (EI)
    • the Quebec Parental Insurance Plan (QPIP)
    • the Canada Pension Plan (CPP), and
    • the Quebec Pension Plan (QPP)
    for an eligible employee for each whole week in the claim period that the eligible employee was on leave with pay.
  • If the eligible employer’s revenue reduction rate and its top-up rate for the period are both 0%, this amount is $0.

Calculation for employees on leave with pay for periods 7 to 9

For periods 7 and later, the calculation of subsidy for employees on leave with pay will be determined by a regulation. This regulation will be released in the future.
