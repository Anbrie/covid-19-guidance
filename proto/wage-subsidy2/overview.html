How the subsidy works

Eligible employers who had any drop in revenue can now qualify for the wage subsidy, starting with the claim periods that began July 5.

July 5 to December 19, 2020

Changes to CEWS that start in claim period 5:

  • the subsidy rate is now variable, based on how much your revenue dropped
  • you no longer need at least a 30% revenue drop to qualify, so you can keep getting the subsidy as employees return to work and your income improves
  • employers who had a drop in revenue of at least 50% over a period of three months get an extra top-up
  • you no longer have to exclude employees who were unpaid for 14 or more days
  • you can use the current period’s CEWS rate or the previous period’s, whichever works in your favour
  • for periods 5 and 6, you can use the old rules and calculation if it works in your favour (the “safe harbour” option)
  • the rate of subsidy will decline beginning in period 7, as business returns to normal

Download infographic: Take another look at the CEWS

March 15 to July 4, 2020

For claim periods 1 to 4 (March 15 to July 4, 2020):

  • you must meet a minimum of 15% (period 1) or 30% (periods 2 to 4) revenue drop to qualify for the subsidy
  • if you qualify for a period, you automatically qualify for the following period
  • the subsidy rate is 75% of eligible employees' remuneration, up to a maximum of $847/week per eligible employee
  • employees who were unpaid for 14 or more consecutive days in the period can't be included

How much you can qualify for

Your base rate is a standard 60%

This means:

  • Eligible employees earning $1,129/week or more would get $1,129 x 60% = $677/week
  • Eligible employees earning less than $1,129/week would get 60% of their remuneration, up to $677/week.

Note:

  • Claim periods 5 and 6 have a maximum base rate of 60%
  • Claim periods 7 to 9 maximum base rates will be reduced from 60% to 20%

Your base rate 1.2 times your revenue drop %

This means:

  • If you had 20% revenue drop, your base rate is 20% x 1.2 = 24%
  • Eligible employees earning $1,129/week or more would get $1,129 x 24% = $271/week
  • Eligible employees earning less than $1,129/week would get 24% of their remuneration, up to $271/week.

Maximizing the subsidy

As long as you meet all eligibility requirements, you can switch between calculation models, per claim period, and per employee.

How you apply

  1. Determine if you are an eligible employer
  2. Determine which employees you can claim the subsidy for
  3. Use the calculator to find out how much subsidy you can claim under the new rules. There are two calculator options:
    • a new web calculator
    • a downloadable spreadsheet
  4. Sign in to your CRA account to input the values of your claim
