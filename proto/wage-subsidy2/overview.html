How it works

All employers who had a revenue loss from July 1, 2020, onward, qualify for CEWS.

July 1 to December 19, 2020

As of July 1, 2020, the CEWS consists of two parts:

  1. a base subsidy available to all eligible employers experiencing a decline in revenues, with the subsidy amount varying depending on the scale of revenue decline; and
  2. a top-up subsidy of up to an additional 25% for employers most adversely affected by Covid-19 (those with more than a 50% revenue drop).
March 15 to June 30, 2020

Employers were previously subject to a 30% revenue decline test, which made them eligible for a payroll subsidy of 75% (to a weekly maximum of $847).

A transition from the old model to the new model

For the period 5 (July 5 to August 1) and period 6 (August 2 to August 29), a safe harbour provision allows for a business that would have been better off under the prior rules (i.e., 75% wage top-up for revenue declines of 30% or more) to receive that prior subsidy amount.

    How you apply

    1. Determine if you are an eligible employer
    2. Determine which employees you can claim
    3. Calculate your subsidy amount using the new web form or using the downloadable spreadsheet
    4. Sign-in to your CRA account to input the values of your claim

    How the subsidy helps

    CEWS helps businesses keep employees on the payroll and encourages employers to re-hire workers previously laid off, and better positions businesses to bounce back following the crisis.
