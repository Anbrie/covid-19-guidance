Eligibility criteria

To be eligible to receive the wage subsidy, you must all three of the following criteria:

  1. Have had a CRA payroll account on March 15, 2020

    Any payroll account set-up after March 15, 2020 is not eligible for the subsidy

  2. Be one of the following types of employers

    • individuals (including trusts)
    • corporations that are not exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act)
    • the following persons that are exempt from income tax (Part I of the Income Tax Act):
      • non-profit organizations
      • agricultural organizations
      • boards of trade
      • chambers of commerce
      • non-profit corporations for scientific research and experimental development
      • labour organizations or societies
      • benevolent or fraternal benefit societies or orders
    • registered charities
    • certain Indigenous government-owned corporations that carry on a business
    • partnerships consisting of eligible employers and certain Indigenous governments
    • registered Canadian amateur athletic associations
    • registered journalism organizations
    • private schools or private colleges, and
    • partnerships consisting of eligible employers (including partnerships where at least 50% of the interests in the partnership are held by eligible employers)

  3. Have experienced an eligible reduction in revenue in the claim period

    July 5 to December 19, 2020

    For claims starting July 5, 2020 (claim periods 5 and later):

    There is no minimum amount of revenue drop required to qualify for the subsidy. The amount your revenue has dropped is used when calculating how much subsidy you receive for these periods.

    You may use the online calculator or spreadsheet to calculate your revenue drop rate for periods 5 and later, including any options available for that claim period, while calculating how much subsidy you may receive.

    or

    You can read the details of how the rate is calculated. Refer to: Revenue drop rate calculation for claim periods 5 and later

    March 15 to July 4, 2020

    For claims from March 15 to July 4, 2020 (claim periods 1 to 4):

    Calculate your reduction by comparing your eligible revenue for the starting month of the claim period with your reference (baseline) revenue. Your reference revenue is either:

    • the revenue you earned in the corresponding month in 2019, or
    • the average of the revenue you earned in January and February, 2020 (alternate prior reference period)

    You must choose one revenue comparison option and use it for all claims you make in periods 1 through 4.

    Note: If you choose the alternate prior reference period (average of your eligible revenue from January and February 2020), you need to check the "prior reference period" election box when completing your CEWS application form.

    Required revenue reduction

    For periods 1 through 4, you must show that your eligible revenue dropped by a certain amount to qualify for CEWS. The amount your revenue dropped is not used to determine how much CEWS you will receive for these periods.

    For period 1, you need to show a drop in eligible revenue between your reference period (March 2019 or the average of January and February 2020) and claim period (March 2020) of at least 15%.

    For periods 2, 3, and 4, you need to show a drop in eligible revenue between your reference period and claim period of at least 30%.

    Automatically qualifying for later periods

    For periods 1, 2, and 3, if you determine that you qualify for the CEWS for one claim period, you automatically qualify for the following claim period and do not need to recalculate your eligible revenue reduction.

    [Confirm:]If you determine that you qualify for the CEWS in period 4, you do not automatically qualify for period 5. You must determine your eligible revenue reduction for period 5 if you intend to make a claim for that period.] New

    What is eligible revenue

    Eligible revenue generally includes revenue earned in Canada from:

    • selling goods
    • rendering services, and
    • others' use of your resources

    Use your normal accounting method when calculating revenue. You can use the cash method or the accrual method, but you must use the same approach throughout.

    Special revenue circumstances

    Registered charities and non-profit organizations

    Registered charities and non-profit organizations may choose whether or not to include revenue from government sources, such as grants, when applying for the subsidy. You must take the same approach for each period you are applying for.

    Read more about eligible revenue for charities and non-profit organizations

    Non-arm's length transactions and affiliated groups

    There are special rules for revenue related to certain non-arm's-length transactions and affiliated groups.

    Read more about calculating eligible revenue in specific situations

Verifying your eligibility

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) will verify your eligibility to receive the CEWS. If you do not meet the eligibility requirements for a period, you will be required to repay any amounts you received for that period. Canadians can also report suspected CEWS misuse through the CRA’s Leads program.
