Official global travel advisory

Avoid non-essential travel outside of Canada until further notice

Issued: March 14, 2020

Many countries have travel or border restrictions, movement restrictions, or quarantines.

Airlines have cancelled flights.

New restrictions may be imposed with little warning.

Your travel plans may be severely disrupted and you may be forced to remain outside of Canada longer than expected.

What you should do

  • Avoid all travel on cruise ships
  • Contact your airline or tour operator to cancel or postpone your trip

If you are outside of Canada

  • Find out what commercial options are still available to return to Canada
  • Consider returning to Canada earlier than planned if these options become more limited
  • Ensure that you have sufficient finances and necessities, including medication, in case your travels are disrupted

Before you go

Travel outside Canada is not advised. If you must travel, do the following:

Prepare for your travel

  • Check your destination for advice on:
    • Safety and security
    • Entry/exit requirements
    • Health
  • Register your travel abroad to receive emergency updates from the Government of Canada
  • Have sufficient finances and necessities, like medication, if travel is disrupted
  • Know what to do if you become sick or suspect exposure to COVID-19 when you're away

Expect changing international measures

  • Expect increased health screening measures at points of entry for international destinations, including airports
  • Local control measures may suddenly change, including movement restrictions such as quarantines
  • Your one-week trip may become much longer
  • You may have reduced access to quality health care

While you're away

At your destination

  • Monitor your health
  • Monitor the news
  • Follow instructions of local authorities if you're in an affected area
  • Monitor travel advice and advisories for your destination

Before leaving your destination

Do not get on board any form of public transportation if you develop symptoms of coronavirus before you are scheduled to leave.

If you experience symptoms or suspect you were exposed to COVID-19

  • Call ahead to a health professional
  • Inform the health care provider or local health authority about symptoms and travel history

Arriving in Canada (airports and border crossings)

What Canada is doing

  • Measures have been implemented at 10 airports

    Airports with COVID-19 measures

    • Vancouver International Airport
    • Toronto-Pearson International Airport
    • Montréal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau International Airport
    • Calgary International Airport
    • Edmonton International Airport
    • Winnipeg Richardson International Airport
    • Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport
    • Ottawa International Airport
    • Québec City Jean Lesage International Airport
    • Halifax Stanfield International Airport
  • The Public Health Agency of Canada has information booths at:
    • Vancouver
    • Toronto-Pearson
    • Montréal

When you arrive

  • Look for messaging on arrivals screens
  • All international travellers will be asked a screening question at electronic kiosks
  • Travellers will receive pamphlets on symptoms and how to contact local health authorities

If you experience symptoms of COVID-19

  • Tell the flight attendant before you land
  • Tell the border services officer as you enter the country
  • A quarantine agent will determine if you should be:
    • transported to hospital to undergo a medical examination
    • reported to the local public health authority

If you suspect you were exposed to COVID-19

  • Tell a border service agent. This is required under the Quarantine Act.

Travellers from Hubei, China, Iran or Italy

Travellers who do not show signs or symptoms of illness upon entry into Canada will be:

  • required to provide contact information
  • given a handout advising them to limit contact with others for 14 days
  • asked to contact their public health authority within 24 hours of arriving in Canada
  • given a surgical mask with instructions on use

Monitoring your health after travel

During the 14 days after your return

  • Monitor your health for COVID-19 symptoms fever, cough and difficulty breathing
  • Take actions that will reduce the spread COVID-19

If you experience symptoms of COVID-19

  • Self-isolate and stay at home
  • Call your public health authority
  • Describe your symptoms and document your travel history
  • Your health care professional or health authority will provide instructions for you to follow
