Find out if you can travel to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - Transit

Effective date: 2020-06-29

You indicated that you are:

  • a foreign national
  • not showing signs and symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • only transiting through Canada
  • on a vessel crossing Canadian water

Based on your answers, you will may be allowed to enter Canada. You cannot enter Canadian waters for an optional reason, such as a touring, sightseeing or pleasure fishing.

What you need to do next

  • have proof your entry into Canadian waters is necessary to transit directly from one place outside Canada to another place outside Canada
  • follow any provincial or territorial requirements, depending where you are transiting through

While in Canada, you must agree to:

  • follow the most direct, continuous (uninterrupted) reasonable route
  • follow mandatory physical distancing and mask requirements
  • comply with anchoring and quarantine requirements
  • report if you land on Canadian soil

If you meet the above conditions, and have all necessary travel documents, you will likely be allowed entry to transit through Canadian waters to reach your final destination.

This answer does not guarantee entry into Canada

  • Final determination regarding entry is made by a government representative at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time of entry into Canada.
  • You must meet the entry requirements under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act and provide appropriate travel and immigration documentation.
  • Failure to comply with the COVID-19 Emergency order is an offence under the Quarantine Act and could lead to imprisonment and/or fines.
