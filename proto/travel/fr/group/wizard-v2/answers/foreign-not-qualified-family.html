Find out if you can travel to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - family - not immediate family

Effective date: 2020-06-29

You indicated that you are:

  • a foreign national
  • not showing signs and symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • reuniting with family in Canada
  • you do not qualify as an immediate family member

You will not be allowed to enter Canada to visit family.

To be considered an immediate family member you must be the:

  • spouse or common-law partner of the person
  • dependent child of the person
  • dependent child of a dependent child of the person
  • parent or step-parent of the person or the;
  • guardian or tutor of the person

For foreign nationals, only these relationships are considered immediate family. Any other relationship (like brother, sister, aunt, adult child who is no longer dependant, etc.) will not be allowed entry for the purpose of reuniting with family.

If you are trying to enter Canada for a different family related reason, see Caring for others, funerals and family support.
