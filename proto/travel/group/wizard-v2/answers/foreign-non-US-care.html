Find out if you can travel to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - Non-U.S. entry - Caring for a family member

Effective date: 2020-06-29

You indicated that you are:

  • a foreign national
  • not showing signs and symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • not coming directly from the United States
  • providing care for a family member (as a non-health professional)

You must meet the entry requirements under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act and provide appropriate travel and immigration documentation. Final determination regarding entry is made by a government representative at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time of entry into Canada. Failure to comply with the COVID-19 Emergency order is an offence under the Quarantine Act and could lead to imprisonment and/or fines.

Based on your answers, you must prove your entry is non-optional to provide care for a family member who must have your assistance, and has no other care options.

You must:

  • have compelling proof your reason for entry is not optional and get a written exemption from IRCC
  • follow a mandatory quarantine period of 14 days
  • have a suitable place to quarantine
  • comply with additional provincial or territorial requirements
