Find out if you can travel to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - Transit

Effective date: 2020-06-29

You indicated that you are:

  • a foreign national
  • not showing signs and symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • only transiting through Canada
  • transiting by land (between Alaska and the contiguous United States)

You must meet the entry requirements under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act and provide appropriate travel and immigration documentation. Final determination regarding entry is made by a government representative at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time of entry into Canada. Failure to comply with the COVID-19 Emergency order is an offence under the Quarantine Act and could lead to imprisonment and/or fines.

Based on your answers, you will likely be allowed into Canada.

If you are travelling from Alaska to the lower 48 states, entry is not limited to a specific port of entry.

While in Canada, you must:

  • if going north, enter at one of five specific ports of entry
  • hang a vehicle tag
  • follow a designated route to your entry point back into the U.S., avoiding all visit national parks, leisure sites or tourism activities
  • comply with mandatory physical distancing and masks during transit
  • confirm your exit from Canada to the nearest CBSA port of entry before entering the U.S.

You may have to follow certain provincial or territorial requirements, depending on your route.
