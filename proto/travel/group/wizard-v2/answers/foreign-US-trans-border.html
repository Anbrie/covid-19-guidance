Find out if you can travel to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - Essential service

Effective date: 2020-06-29

You indicated that you are:

  • a foreign national
  • not showing signs and symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • coming directly from the United States
  • part of a trans-border community (border crossing is essential to everyday functions)

Based on your answers, you will likely be allowed to enter Canada.

Being part of a trans-border community, or where crossing is essential essential for everyday functions (e.g. buying groceries, gas, attending medical appointments), is generally exempt from quarantine for situations described under trans-border communities or everyday functions.

What you need to do next

  • bring any proof you have to support your reason for entry
  • read the provincial or territorial requirements

The following are not considered quarantine exempt crossings

  • cross-border child custody arrangements
  • attending class as a cross-border student (exemptions no longer apply as of August 2020)
If a government representative at the border determines you are not quarantine exempt

You must:

  • read and follow the mandatory quarantine requirements
  • make sure you have a suitable place and plan to quarantine

You may request a letter of interpretation to assess whether future travels may be exempt from quarantine.
