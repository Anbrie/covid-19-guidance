Find out if you can travel to Canada - Foreign national without symptoms - Student - Non-US

Effective date: 2020-06-29

You indicated that you are:

  • a foreign national
  • not showing signs and symptoms consistent with COVID-19
  • not coming directly from the United States
  • an international student

Based on your answers, you must prove it is essential that you need to enter Canada for your studies and have a valid study permit, or were approved for a study permit on or before March 18, 2020.

If you have compelling proof and valid study permit, you will likely be allowed to enter Canada.

Acceptable reasons to enter Canada for your studies:

  • you’re already living in Canada and are returning
  • you need to be in Canada for your program (for laboratories, workshops)
  • your school does not have an online option
  • your school does have an online option, but it is not possible from your home country (due to internet restrictions, bandwidth limitation, or timezone differences where you would be awake at unreasonable hours)

What you need to do next

  • read and follow the mandatory quarantine requirements
  • make sure you have a suitable place and plan to quarantine
  • read the provincial or territorial requirements
Students travelling with immediate family

If you are travelling with immediate family, such as your spouse or a dependent child, they may be allowed to enter Canada with you. They will need to meet the same requirements and proof for entry as a foreign nationals reuniting with immediate family

.

Students in a health field: quarantine exemptions

If you are working in Canada as a student in a health field, you will likely be exempt from quarantine as long as you do not directly care for persons 65 years of age or older within the 14-day period that begins on the day you enter Canada.

You must still comply with any additional provincial or territorial requirements.

This answer does not guarantee entry into Canada

  • Final determination regarding entry is made by a government representative at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time of entry into Canada.
  • You must meet the entry requirements under the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act and provide appropriate travel and immigration documentation.
  • Failure to comply with the COVID-19 Emergency order is an offence under the Quarantine Act and could lead to imprisonment and/or fines.
