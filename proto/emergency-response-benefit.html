Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB)

The Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB) supports Canadians who have lost their income because of COVID-19.

It supports individuals who may or may not have become unemployed, but who:

  • have lost their income because there is no longer work for them
  • can’t work because they are sick
  • have to stay home to take care of their children.

The CERB is not Employment Insurance (EI). It is a separate benefit that you may get on its own, or in addition to EI.

If you are already getting EI:

  • You will continue to get your EI payments
  • If your EI benefits end before October 3, you can apply for the CERB if you can’t return to work because of COVID-19

If you already applied for EI on or after March 15:

  • You do not need to reapply
  • You will automatically get the CERB first
  • You will get your regular EI benefits after your 16 weeks of CERB coverage
