How the subsidy is calculated

The CERS provides both a base subsidy and in some cases, a top-up amount.

The base subsidy rate applies to a maximum of $75,000 in eligible expenses per location and an overall maximum of $300,000 in expenses for your business and any affiliated entities per claim period.

If you must close or cease certain activities at one or more of your locations under a public health order for one week or longer, you may be eligible for a top-up subsidy of up to 25% of eligible expenses per affected location.

Your CERS rent subsidy rate is

  • 80% of your revenue drop percentage, up to a maximum of 40%
  • plus Plus (if revenue drop > 50%):
    • your revenue drop percentage minus 50%
    • multiplied by 1.25
    • to a maximum of 25%
  • equals Your CERS rent subsidy rate

Your CERS top-up rate (per business location) is

  • 0% if your base rate is 0%
    • OR
  • 25% (fixed top-up rate)
  • times the number of days your province or municipality was on lock down due to COVID-19
  • divided by 28 (the number of days in the CERS period)
  • equals Your CERS top-up rate
Example top-up rate calculation

For example, if one of your business locations was on lockdown for 7 days of the 28 day period, you would be eligible to receive:

  • 25% base top-up rate multiplied by
  • 7 days out of 28
  • For a 6.25% top-up rate.

Before you start the calculation

Check these boxes to confirm you have all the information you need before you start your calculation:

    • Who can apply

    • Expenses you can claim

    • Find out if you have affiliated entities

      An affiliated entity is an organization that:

      • controls another entity (directly or indirectly)
      • is controlled by another entity (directly or indirectly)
      • is under common control alongside another entity

      Although the rules can be complicated, some examples include:

      • you run your business activities through more than one entity (corporations, trusts or partnerships)
      • you and your spouse each own or operate a business, directly or through a corporation or other entity
      • the entity through which your business or activities are conducted is owned or controlled by people who own or control other entities

You should understand the definitions and gather all the necessary information before you calculate your claim.

You can now proceed to calculate the amounts you will need for your claim.

Public health restrictions and business activities

To qualify for the top-up:

  • your business location is subject to a federal, provincial or municipal COVID-19 public health order
  • approximately 25% or more of your revenue during the prior reference period came from business activities that you are not allowed to carry out under the restrictions
Example: Take out restaurant

Your take out restaurant was in a location restricted by a provincial lock down due to COVID-19.

However, because activities weren't affected by the lock down, the take out restaurant still does not qualify for the top-up.
