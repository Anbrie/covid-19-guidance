Keep getting your payments

On this page

  • You must re-apply every 2 weeks
  • When to expect your payment

You must re-apply every 2 weeks

The CRB does not renew automatically. To keep getting your payments, you must re-apply every 2 week. You may only apply after the [#]-week period you are applying for has passed. To find the earliest day to re-apply for each period, refer to: When to apply

Make sure you're still eligible

You must continue to meet all the CRB eligibility criteria to keep receiving the benefit. Check that you are still eligible every time you re-apply.

Continue to look for work

The Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB) gives support to those who are unable to work or have reduced income due to COVID-19. You must be looking for work or return to work when your employer asks you back, provided you are able and it is reasonable to do so. We encourage you to consult Job Bank, Canada’s national employment service that offers tools to help with your job search.

How to re-apply

To keep getting the CRB, you must apply again for each period. The application process is the same every time. If you know when to apply, apply online or by phone

If you are not sure when to apply for the next eligible period, go to when to apply

When to expect your payment

If you are eligible for the CRB, you can expect to get $800 for the 2-week period you applied for.

Direct deposit
Direct deposit takes 3 business days if you have it set up with the CRA.
By mail
A mailed cheque should arrive in about 10 business days.
