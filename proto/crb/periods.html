How eligibility periods work

A Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB) eligibility period is a specific 2-week period. When you apply, you will receive an $800 ($720 after taxes) payment for the period that you applied for.

The CRB does not renew automatically. You must apply for each period between August 30, 2020 and August 28, 2021 separately. You can apply for a maximum of 13 periods.

Periods only last 2 weeks

Each eligibility period is a 2-week period with a specific start and end date. You must apply in arrears, that is after the 2 weeks have passed.

You can only apply for 1 period at a time. If your situation continues, you must re-apply for another 2-week period.

Eligibility ends August 28, 2021

The CRB terminates after you have received it for a maximum of 13 periods (26 weeks). You may apply for benefits retroactively for any 2-week period until October 28, 2021. After that, the CRA will not accept any applications.
