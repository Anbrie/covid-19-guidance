How much you can get

How we calculate your payment

The Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB) provides a payment of $800 (before taxes) for each 2-week period that you apply for. Due to a 10% tax withheld at source by the CRA, the actual payment you get is $720 per 2-week period.

Example

  • $800 (CRB gross amount)
  • minus   $80 (10% tax withheld at source)
  • equals $720 (amount you receive per period)

If you continue to be eligible, the CRB is available for a maximum of 13 periods between September 27, 2020 and August 28, 2021.

Impact on your taxes

A 10% tax is withheld at source. But you must still report CRB payments that you receive as income when you file your personal income tax for 2020.

The CRA will provide you with a T4A tax slip at time tax for this purpose.

How your income affects what you keep

You may earn employment or self-employment income while you receive the CRB. But to make sure the benefit reaches those who need it most, there is an earnings limit of $38,000 for the 2020 tax year. This limit excludes CRB payments.

You will have to reimburse $0.50 of the CRB for every dollar of net income you earned above $38,000 on your 2020 income tax return.

Taxable income Reimbursement
If your taxable income is over $38,000 You will have to reimburse $0.50 of the benefit for every dollar of net income that you earned above $38,000
If your taxable income is $38,000 or less You will not have to reimburse the CRB

Taxable income includes: All amounts that are normally considered part of net income for income tax purposes. It includes any CERB payments you received. It does not include other emergency benefits (EI, Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit, or Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit).
