Apply for CERB and EI together

You should apply for either EI Regular or EI Sickness benefits if you are eligible. This would let you get the maximum benefit available to you for the longest period of time.

You will receive the CERB first. Once CERB ends, you do not need to re-apply for EI. If you apply for EI later, it could cause a break in benefits to you.

After CERB ends, how long you might get EI for, and the amount, would depend on where you live and how long you have been unemployed for.

You may be entitled to EI regular benefits if you:

  • were an employee or self-employed
  • were a full-time employee for at least (5) months last year (30 or more hours per week)
  • were a part-time employee for at least (8) months last year (less than 30 hours per week)

Apply for EI Regular benefits and CERB

You may be entitled to Employment Insurance (EI) Sickness benefits if:

  • you're unable to work for medical reasons
  • your regular weekly earnings from work have decreased by more than 40% for at least one week
  • you accumulated 600 insured hours* of work in the 52 weeks before the start of your claim, or since the start of your last claim, whichever is shorter

Apply for EI Sickness benefits and CERB

Not eligible for EI? Get ready to apply to CERB

Applications for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit will begin the week of Monday, April 6, 2020.

The CRA cannot accept applications before then.

Get ready to apply

Although you can't apply yet, you should get ready by making sure you have a CRA account and updated profile:

Do you have a CRA My Account?

  • Yes - I have a CRA My Account

    Make sure your direct deposit and mailing information is up to date with the CRA.

    Applications open on April 6.

    Sign in to your CRA account

  • Yes - but I'm locked out

    To recover your CRA My Account and apply for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit, you will have to call and get a new security code.

    Contact CRA to recover your account

  • No - but I have a My Service Canada Account for EI

    Sign in to CRA My Account with a My Service Canada Account

    Since you have a My Service Canada Account, you do not need to register for a CRA My Account.

    After you sign in to My Service Canada Account:

    1. Look for the link to "Switch to Canada Revenue Agency" and then "agree" on the Registration and authentication page
    2. Update your address and direct deposit with CRA (CRA does not have your personal information from your My Service Canada Account)
    3. You can then complete the application when it becomes available on April 6th.

    Sign in to My Service Canada Account

  • No - I don't have either

    Register for a CRA My Account to apply for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit

    Register for CRA My Account

How to apply after April 6th

You must apply on a specific day

Whether you apply online or by phone, we want to provide the best service possible to everyone. To help manage this, we have set up specific days when you may apply. Please use the following guidelines:

  • You can apply on Mondays if you were born January to June
  • You can apply on Tuesdays if you were born July to December
  • You can apply on Wednesday to Sunday regardless of when you were born
Online:

Sign into your CRA account

Instructions once you're in your CRA account
  1. Go to COVID-19: Canada Emergency Response Benefit in the alert banner at the top of the page
  2. Select the period you want to apply for
  3. Declare that you qualify for the benefit
  4. Confirm we have the right payment information
By phone:
Call the automated toll-free line
Before you call

To verify your identity, you'll need

  • your social insurance number (SIN)
  • mailing address

If you are calling the CRA on behalf of someone else, you must be an authorized representative

Telephone number

1-800-959-2019

Instructions
  1. Select your language preference: English or French
  2. Follow the prompts to enter your information, including:
    • your SIN
    • confirmation of your mailing address
    • the period you are applying for
  3. Declare that you qualify for the benefit

The CRA will issue payments by direct deposit or cheque, based on the payment method you have on file.

Hours
Always open (automated line)

Apply again every 4 weeks

You will need to apply and confirm your eligibility for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit every 4 weeks if your situation continues.
